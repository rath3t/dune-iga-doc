Computes the second derivatives of the local view.

Parameters
[in]uThe parameter value of the local view on the geometry
Returns
A tuple of the global position, the Jacobian matrix and Hessian matrix

Example curve on surface

The local view contains a parameterSpaceGeometry, which describes the composition function

\[g: \begin{cases}\mathbb{R} \rightarrow \mathbb{R}^2 \\ t \mapsto g(t) \end{cases},\]

which describes the curve's local parametrization in terms of the patch geometries parametrization The patch geometry is described by

\[f: \begin{cases}\mathbb{R}^2 \rightarrow \mathbb{R}^3 \\ (u,v) \mapsto * f(u,v) \end{cases},\]

which describes a surface in 3D. Then the curve's realization on the surface in the 3D space can be written by

\[h: \begin{cases}\mathbb{R} \rightarrow \mathbb{R}^3 \\ t \mapsto f(g_1(t),g_2(t)) \end{cases},\]

This function calculates the Jacobian and Hessian matrices for the composition function \(h(g_1(t), g_2(t))\) where \(g(t)\) is the original curve and \(h(u, v)\) is the patch geometry function. The computation involves chain and product rules between \(g(t)\) and \(h(u, v)\).

  • The Jacobian matrix J is given by

    \[ J = \frac{\partial f }{\partial g_1} \frac{\partial g_1}{\partial t} + \frac{\partial f}{\partial g_2} \frac{\partial g_2}{\partial t} \]

    and has dimensions \(3\times 1\) or in general \(\verb+worlddimension+\times \verb+mydimension+\).

The Hessian matrix H is given by:

\[ H = \frac{\partial^2 f}{\partial g_1\partial g_1} \left(\frac{\partial g_1}{\partial t}\right)^2 +\frac{\partial^2 f}{\partial g_2\partial g_2} \left(\frac{\partial g_2}{\partial t}\right)^2 + 2\frac{\partial^2 f}{\partial g_1\partial g_2} \frac{\partial g_1}{\partial t} \frac{\partial g_2}{\partial t} + \frac{\partial f}{\partial g_1}\frac{\partial^2 g_1}{\partial t^2}+ \frac{\partial f}{\partial g_2}\frac{\partial^2 g_2}{\partial t^2} \]

and has dimensions \(1\times 3\) or in general \(\verb|mydimension*(mydimension+1)/2| \times \verb+worlddimension+\).
